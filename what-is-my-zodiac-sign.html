How to Find Your Zodiac Sign

Your zodiac sign (also called sun sign) is determined by the position of the sun at the time of your birth. Because the sun moves through each sign on roughly the same dates every year, you only need your birth month and day to find your sign. This calculator uses the traditional Western zodiac dates, accurate for most years. For cusp births (dates near the transition), the exact year may shift the sign by a day – but this tool still gives you the correct sign for over 99% of cases.

Why Your Sun Sign Matters

Your sun sign represents your core identity – your ego, willpower, and conscious self. It's the most popular dimension of astrology because it's easy to calculate and gives a broad overview of personality. Understanding your sign can help you recognize your strengths, weaknesses, and natural tendencies. This self‑awareness can improve relationships, career choices, and personal growth.

Remember: astrology is a tool for reflection, not a rigid prediction. Every person is unique, and your full birth chart (including moon and rising signs) provides a more complete picture.

Frequently Asked Questions

What if I was born on a cusp?
If your birthday is within a day of a sign change, the exact year and time matter. This calculator uses the standard date ranges. For critical precision, consult an ephemeris or professional astrologer.
Can my zodiac sign change over time?
No. Your sun sign is fixed for life based on your birth date. However, there is a phenomenon called "Ophiuchus" that some media discuss, but Western astrology does not use it.
Do I need my birth time to find my sun sign?
No, only the date (month and day) is required. Birth time is needed for rising sign and moon sign, but not for sun sign.
Is this calculator accurate for leap years?
Yes. The zodiac dates are based on the sun's ecliptic longitude, not the Gregorian calendar. Our simple month/day logic works correctly for all years.
