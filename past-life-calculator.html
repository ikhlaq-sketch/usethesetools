A Brief History of Past Life Beliefs

The idea of reincarnation – that the soul is reborn into new bodies over multiple lifetimes – is ancient. It appears in Hinduism (as samsara) as early as 1500 BCE, and in Buddhism and Jainism. The Greek philosopher Pythagoras (c. 570–495 BCE) also taught the transmigration of souls. In the West, reincarnation was widely accepted by Greek and Roman thinkers, including Plato and the gnostic Christians, but was suppressed during the early Middle Ages. The concept resurfaced in the 19th century with the Theosophical Society and has since become a popular spiritual concept, especially in New Age circles. Psychiatrist Ian Stevenson (1918–2007) conducted extensive research on children who claimed to remember past lives, publishing hundreds of cases. While not scientifically proven, many people find value in exploring past lives as a tool for self‑understanding and healing.
