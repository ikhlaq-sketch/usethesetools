What Is the Chinese Zodiac?

The Chinese zodiac is a 12‑year cycle, each year represented by an animal. Unlike Western astrology, it's based on the lunar calendar. Your animal sign influences personality, career, and relationships according to Chinese tradition.

The 12 Animals and Their Meanings

Rat: Quick-witted, resourceful, adaptable.
Ox: Diligent, reliable, strong.
Tiger: Brave, competitive, unpredictable.
Rabbit: Gentle, elegant, cautious.
Dragon: Confident, intelligent, enthusiastic.
Snake: Wise, mysterious, intuitive.
Horse: Energetic, independent, impatient.
Goat: Calm, creative, empathetic.
Monkey: Clever, witty, curious.
Rooster: Observant, hardworking, honest.
Dog: Loyal, honest, protective.
Pig: Generous, compassionate, diligent.

Each animal also cycles through five elements (Wood, Fire, Earth, Metal, Water), which further refines personality.

FAQs

Is Chinese zodiac same as Western zodiac?
No – Chinese zodiac is based on lunar years and 12 animals. Western is based on solar months and constellations.
What if I was born in January or February?
The Chinese New Year varies between Jan 21 and Feb 20. If your birthday falls before the lunar new year, your animal is the previous year's.
